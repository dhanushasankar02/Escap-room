Safety First, Fun Second

Your well-being is our top priority. We maintain strict safety protocols to ensure an unforgettable and secure experience for everyone.

Watch Briefing

Target Safety Zones

Understanding where to stand is the most important part of axe throwing. Our lanes are clearly marked to keep you safe.

Before you pick up an axe, our certified coaches will walk you through the lane layout. Please familiarize yourself with the color-coded markings on the floor to ensure a safe environment for everyone.

  • The Red Line: The throwing line. Never cross this line unless retrieving your axe.
  • The Blue Zone: The spectator area. All non-throwers must remain behind.
  • Retrieval Protocol: Only one person may retrieve an axe from the target at a time.
  • Coach Signals: Always wait for your coach's clear signal before beginning.

The Golden Rules of Axe Throwing

1. The Stance

Keep both feet firmly planted behind the red fault line. Never cross the line until your axe has come to a complete stop.

2. The Grip

Hold the axe with a firm, two-handed grip at the base of the handle. Keep the blade perfectly straight.

3. The Release

Bring the axe straight back over your head and release when your arms are fully extended forward. Do not flick your wrists.

Escape Room Protocol

Our rooms are challenging, but they are never dangerous. Follow these rules to ensure a smooth.

  • Doors are never locked: You can exit the room at any time using the marked push-bars entire.
  • 24/7 Monitoring: Game Masters watch and listen to your entire session at all times room.
  • No Brute Force: If it doesn't open easily, you haven't found the right clue. Never use force.

Dress Code & Gear

What to wear (and what to leave at home).

Mandatory

Closed-toe shoes are absolutely required. No sandals, flip-flops, or high heels permitted.

Prohibited

No loose clothing that could catch on equipment. Outside axes and props are strictly forbidden.

First Aid & Medical Preparedness

We are fully equipped to handle any minor injuries and emergencies on-site.
